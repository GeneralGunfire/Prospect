# Product

## Register

product

## Users

South African high school students in Grades 10-12, ages 15-18, choosing careers and navigating post-school life. Primarily mobile users on limited data plans (many on prepaid). Many are first-generation university students with no guidance at home. Teachers and parents use it as a secondary audience to support students. Users are under real pressure — matric exams, APS scores, bursary deadlines — and need clear, fast, trustworthy information.

## Product Purpose

Prospect SA is a career guidance and civic education platform. It helps young South Africans discover careers that match their interests (via RIASEC quiz), understand what subjects and APS scores they need, find bursaries and NSFAS funding, access free matric study resources, navigate government procedures (civics), understand taxes and cost of living, and engage with community issues (potholes, water). Success looks like a Grade 11 student who arrives knowing nothing about their options and leaves with a clear path, applied for funding, and confidence about what comes next.

## Brand Personality

Practical. Empowering. Clear.

The voice is direct and respectful — not condescending, not corporate. Like a brilliant older sibling who has been through the system and explains it plainly. Never jargon-heavy. Never government-speak. Urgency without panic. Confidence without arrogance.

## Anti-references

- SA government department websites (cluttered, inaccessible, overwhelming)
- Corporate recruitment portals (impersonal, formal, intimidating to a 16-year-old)
- Over-designed SaaS dashboards (feature-heavy, metric-obsessed, wrong context)
- Cluttered job boards (noise, low relevance, no educational layer)
- EdTech platforms that feel like homework (boring greys, passive content, no agency)

Positive references: Khan Academy's lesson clarity and progression. Yenza Careers' warm SA-context color approach. Linear's clean product UI discipline. MTN's accessibility-first mobile approach for low-bandwidth users.

## Design Principles

1. **Mobile-first always** — most users are on phones with limited data. Every UI decision must work at 375px with one thumb. No heavy images, no decorative flourishes that cost bandwidth.
2. **Clarity over cleverness** — if a 15-year-old who just opened the app can't understand what to do next in 3 seconds, it's too complex. Hierarchy before color. Labels before icons.
3. **Respect the user's time and data** — fast loads, data saver mode, no unnecessary animation, lazy images. Every kilobyte is a cost to someone.
4. **Earn trust with information** — accurate, up-to-date SA-specific data builds confidence. Wrong bursary amounts or outdated APS tables destroy it. Real info, real sources.
5. **Empowerment through clarity** — government processes, career paths, and funding feel intimidating. Prospect's job is to make them feel navigable. Warm, structured, step-by-step.

## Accessibility and Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. High-contrast text on all backgrounds — many users are reading in direct sunlight on older phone screens. Touch targets minimum 44px. Avoid red/green-only status signals (colorblind awareness). Support reduced motion. Text must be readable at system font-size increases. No information conveyed by color alone.
